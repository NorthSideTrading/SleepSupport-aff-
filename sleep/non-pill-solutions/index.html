How to Choose the Right Solution

Different non-pill solutions address different types of snoring:

  • Mouthpieces (MAD): Best for jaw-related snoring. Gently advance the jaw to open the airway.
  • Nasal strips/dilators: Help with nasal congestion-related snoring by opening nasal passages.
  • Wedge pillows: Address positional snoring by elevating the head and opening the airway.

Consider your primary snoring pattern, comfort preferences, and any jaw or dental sensitivities when choosing.

Who Shouldn't Use These Solutions

Consult a healthcare provider before using oral devices if you have:

  • TMJ (temporomandibular joint) disorders
  • Severe dental problems or loose teeth
  • Sleep apnea symptoms (breathing interruptions during sleep)
  • Jaw pain or stiffness

For severe snoring or suspected sleep apnea, see a sleep specialist for proper evaluation and treatment.

Fitting & Usage Tips

MAD Fitting
Follow manufacturer instructions for boil-and-bite fitting. Start with minimal advancement and adjust gradually for comfort.
Adjustment Period
Most people need 3-7 nights to adjust. Start with shorter wear periods and increase gradually as comfort improves.
Daily Care
Rinse with cool water after use. Clean with a soft brush and mild soap. Allow to air dry completely before storage.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I choose the right non-pill solution?
Consider your snoring type (mouth vs nasal), comfort preferences, and trial/return policies. MADs work for jaw-related snoring, nasal strips for nasal congestion, and wedge pillows for positional snoring.
Who shouldn't use oral devices?
People with TMJ disorders, severe dental issues, or loose teeth should consult a dentist before using MADs. Those with severe sleep apnea should see a sleep specialist.
How long does it take to get used to a mouthpiece?
Most people need 3-7 nights to adjust to wearing an oral device. Start with shorter periods and gradually increase wear time as comfort improves.
Can I use multiple solutions together?
Some combinations work well, like nasal strips with a wedge pillow. However, avoid using multiple oral devices together. Consult your healthcare provider for complex snoring patterns.
