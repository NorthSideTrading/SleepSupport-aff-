How to Choose an Anti-Snoring Device

Jaw Position

MAD devices advance your lower jaw forward. Start with minimal advancement (1-2mm) and adjust gradually to find your comfort zone.

Mouth Breathing

If you breathe through your mouth, look for devices with breathing holes or consider nasal strips as an additional solution.

Sleep Position

Side sleepers may need less jaw advancement than back sleepers. Consider your primary sleep position when selecting a device.

Important Considerations

Anti-snoring mouthpieces may help reduce snoring for some adults. They are not a treatment for sleep apnea or other medical conditions.

Avoid if you have: TMJ/jaw disorders, loose teeth, dentures, recent dental work, or severe respiratory conditions. Consider professional care if you have severe symptoms or breathing interruptions.

Device FAQ

Mouthpiece vs nasal strips: which is better?

Mouthpieces target jaw and tongue position, while nasal strips open nasal passages. For mouth-based snoring, mouthpieces are typically more effective. Some people use both together.

How long does adaptation take?

Most users need 3-7 nights to adjust to wearing a mouthpiece. Start with minimal jaw advancement and gradually increase. Some jaw soreness initially is normal but should not cause pain.

How do I clean my mouthpiece?

Rinse daily with cool water and brush gently with a soft toothbrush. Avoid alcohol-based mouthwashes, bleach, or hot water, which can damage the material.

When should I see a professional?

Consult a healthcare professional if snoring persists, is very loud, includes breathing pauses, or if you experience daytime fatigue. These may indicate sleep apnea requiring medical evaluation.
